Blue Infrastructures Mapping Initiative.

What is Blue Green Infrastructure and why is it important?

Blue Infrastructure is a way to view the water resources around us to see how it best contributes to our needs for preservation of ancient water sources, ecological soundness and healthy drinking water.

Blue Infrastructure are vital sources of water for people living in the Kathmandu Valley. Spout sites and the water they provide also hold important cultural and spiritual meaning.However, spouts are increasingly running dry and many residents worry that spouts without water will become museum pieces. Stone spouts, or dhunge dhara in Nepali and hiti in Newari, are vital sources of water for people living in the Kathmandu Valley. Spout sites and the water they provide also hold important cultural and spiritual meaning.

However, spouts are increasingly running dry and many residents worry that spouts without water will become museum pieces.

Our objectives

The broader objective of this initiative is to mainstream blue and green infrastructure in the development policy of Kathmandu Metropolitan city and other developing cities of Nepal. Following goals are determined to achieve the broader objective of the initiative.

  • Prepare a dynamic inventory through systematic and scientific data collection and engaging the youths in its management through the concept of young citizen scientists.
  • Develop the user friendly, dynamic and comprehensive web platform to visualize the BGI within the city, and regularly update the information through cloud sourcing
  • Conduct series of research, policy dialogues, and share the best practices on BGI bringing policy makers, decision makers, researchers, practitioners, students, youths and community stakeholder in a common platform.
  • Orient the stakeholders to adopt Locally Adapted, Resource-Efficient and Systemic Interventions

Recognitions and Awards

The first scoping phase of this initiative was awarded by the KMC Mayor's Fellowship Research Grant to one of our team members. The study involved the mapping of traditional blue infrastructures like Hiti and ponds.

Blue Green Infrastructure Mapping is a joint initiative of Institute of Himalayan Risk Reduction and NAXA Pvt. Ltd.
