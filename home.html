BGI in Kathmandu Valley

Blue Infrastructure are vital sources of water for people living in the Kathmandu Valley. Spout sites and the water they provide also hold important cultural and spiritual meaning. However, spouts are increasingly running dry and many residents worry that spouts without water will become museum pieces.

Why preserve Blue Infrastructures?

Natural water resources include lakes, rivers, streams, ice pack, groundwater, precipitation and oceans. As global climate change begins to affect the distribution of water resources in the world, water use in all sectors is coming under increasing scrutiny. Residential, commercial and industrial users often compete with agricultural irrigation, hydropower production and navigation for water resources.

  • Preserving Cultural Heritage

    Blue Infrastructures are vital sources of intangible and tangible cultural heritage for Kathmandu residents and are significant religious sites for Buddhists and Hindus.

  • Residential, Commercial and Industrial Use

    Residential water use includes drinking, cleaning etc. In the commercial and industrial sectors, most water is used for processing products, with cooling coming in second.

  • Hydropower and Hydroelectric facilities

    Hydroelectric facilities use the power of flowing water to turn turbines that produce electricity. New technologies promise to increase the efficiency of power generation.
